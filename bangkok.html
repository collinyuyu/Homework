Bangkok

A vibrant metropolis where ancient traditions meet modern innovation, known for its stunning temples, bustling markets, and delicious street food.

Bangkok, Thailand's capital and largest city, is a dynamic urban center situated on the banks of the Chao Phraya River. With a rich cultural heritage and a population of over 8 million, it offers a fascinating blend of historic landmarks and contemporary city life.

Top Attractions

Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn)

An iconic riverside temple known for its stunning Khmer-style spires decorated with colorful porcelain, best photographed at sunrise.

Chao Phraya River, Bangkok Yai

The Grand Palace

A magnificent complex of royal buildings that served as the official residence of Thai kings for over 150 years, featuring exquisite architecture.

Na Phra Lan Road, Phra Nakhon

Chatuchak Weekend Market

One of the world's largest weekend markets with over 15,000 stalls selling everything from clothing and antiques to local handicrafts and street food.

Kamphaeng Phet Road, Chatuchak

Travel Tips for Bangkok

Best Time to Visit

November to February offers cooler temperatures (25-30Â°C) and less humidity, making it ideal for exploring the city.

Getting Around

Use BTS Skytrain and MRT subway to avoid traffic. Tuk-tuks are fun for short trips but always negotiate fares first.

Local Cuisine

Don't miss pad thai, tom yum goong, mango sticky rice, and street food from vendors with long queues of locals.

Temple Etiquette

Cover shoulders and knees when visiting temples. Remove shoes before entering main buildings and dress respectfully.
