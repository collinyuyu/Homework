Seoul

A dynamic metropolis where ancient traditions meet cutting-edge modernity, the vibrant capital of South Korea.

Seoul, the capital and largest city of South Korea, seamlessly blends over 600 years of history with futuristic skyscrapers, K-pop culture, and culinary excellence. As one of the world's top digital cities, it offers a unique contrast between its royal palaces and high-tech innovations.

Top Attractions

Gyeongbokgung Palace

Seoul's main royal palace, built in 1395, featuring stunning architecture, beautiful gardens, and the famous changing of the guard ceremony.

Jongno-gu, Northern Seoul

Myeong-dong

Seoul's premier shopping district, offering everything from luxury brands to street food, with vibrant neon lights day and night.

Jung-gu, Central Seoul

N Seoul Tower

An iconic landmark offering panoramic views of Seoul from Namsan Mountain, famous for its love locks and romantic atmosphere.

Namsan Mountain, Central Seoul

Travel Tips for Seoul

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April-May) for cherry blossoms and autumn (October-November) for pleasant weather and fall foliage.

Getting Around

Efficient subway system covers most attractions. T-Money cards work on all public transport and some convenience stores.

Local Cuisine

Try kimchi jjigae (kimchi stew), bulgogi (marinated beef), tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes), and street food like hotteok.

Cultural Tips

Learn basic Korean phrases as a sign of respect. Remove shoes when entering traditional establishments and some restaurants.
