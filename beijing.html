Beijing

The capital city where ancient history meets modern innovation, home to iconic landmarks and rich cultural heritage.

Beijing, the capital of China, is a city where millennia of history meet cutting-edge modernity. As one of the world's oldest continuously inhabited cities, it boasts an unparalleled collection of historical and cultural sites, while also showcasing China's rapid development and innovation.

Top Attractions

The Great Wall

One of the Seven Wonders of the World, this ancient defensive structure stretches over 13,000 miles and offers breathtaking views.

50 miles northwest of Beijing

Forbidden City

The imperial palace complex served as the home of Chinese emperors for nearly 500 years and is the world's largest ancient palace.

Central Beijing

Temple of Heaven

A UNESCO World Heritage site where emperors of the Ming and Qing dynasties prayed for good harvests.

Southeastern Beijing

Travel Tips for Beijing

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) offer the most pleasant weather.

Getting Around

Beijing has an excellent subway system that's affordable and covers most tourist areas.

Local Cuisine

Try Peking duck, dumplings, and hot pot for an authentic Beijing culinary experience.

Language

Mandarin is the official language, but many in tourism speak basic English.
